Assets and Liabilities allow you to further customize your Contractor by defining their background, personality, and other major characteristics.

Assets are beneficial and cost 3x their point value in Exp. Liabilities are negative and credit you 3x their point value in extra Exp.

They are arranged in four categories: Physical, Mental / Emotional, Background, and Restricted. Restricted Assets and Liabilities require your Playgroup Leader's approval to take.

Traumas and Battle Scars, Conditions and Circumstances.

Assets and Liabilities grant Traumas, Battle Scars, Conditions, and Circumstances. These are ways of tracking long-term statuses that affect your Contractor and are normally obtained as you play.

  • Traumas are specific mental health issues such as phobias, delusions, or compulsions.
    • Contractors are especially resilient to mental health issues and can cure any Trauma by opening up to a therapist (and spending 3 Exp) on a Downtime.
    • Some Gifts also grant the ability to cure Traumas.
  • Battle Scars are significant physical issues that do not heal, such as missing fingers, blindness, or having your face covered in scar tissue from a burn.
    • Battle Scars cannot be cured by mundane means.
    • Some Gifts allow Contractors to cure Battle Scars on themselves or others.
  • Circumstances describe how your Contractor fits into the setting. Things like connections, notoriety, wealth, and legal status are examples of Circumstances.
    • Most circumstances can be changed, but it won't happen for free: you must plan and execute a Move to change your place in the world.
  • Conditions are any additional, significant facts about your Contractor's person, such as dominating personality traits, physical peculiarities, or curses affecting them.
    • The methods of obtaining or ridding yourself of Conditions depends entirely on each particular Condition.
    • Many Conditions are effectively permanent.
