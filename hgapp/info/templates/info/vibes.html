The Mixtape

This Mixtape evokes The Contract.

If you asked any of The Contract's fans to make their own mixtape, they'd probably come up with something completely different. For me, this collection of songs embodies the diversity of Contractors as well as the tone and contemporary nature of the setting.

I'm pretty into lyrics. If you're not really about that, your milage may vary.

Listen on Youtube

Why These Songs?

Wolf Piss by Aesop Rock
So many of Aesop’s songs capture the outsider urban fantasy vibe The Contract evokes. “Wolf Piss” has so many golden nuggets of character, setting, and the supernatural. Sit back and let the barrage of motifs and images wash over you.

Drain the Blood by The Distillers
Like many punk songs, this anthem acknowledges the slimy, shittiness of being on the losing side of society while simultaneously building the outsiders up. “All my friends are murderers” is certainly a sentiment a lot of Contractors share.

The Man by The Killers
Not all Contractors are poor, outsider punks. Some start their careers as successful business people, jaded manipulators, or hungry bullies. Some of them know what they want and are already trying to make it happen. Maybe you can help each other, if you’ve got something to offer.

The Cult of Dionysus by The Orion Experience
Contractors come in many shapes from many backgrounds. Their methods are various and personal, their ambitions global in scale. The joy of The Contract is the ability to play any sort of character, even a would-be hedonistic cult leader. A group of Contractors is a grab-bag of big personalities and conflicting ambitions. They are as unique as snowflakes and thus sometimes referred to as “Snowflakes of carnage.”

Kill V Maim by Grimes
Half of getting power is having the foolishness to use it. Contractors fuck up. Some face consequences, and some get out for free. Love the overconfidence in this one. A lot of Contractors with this attitude end up in the graveyard, but few without it make it far to begin with.

Comfort Eagle by CAKE
This one’s similar to “The Cult of Dionysus” but a bit more cynical. Hey, not all upstart religions are created equal. This one smacks of commerce and media and false prophets, all of which are commonplace in The Illumination.

That Funny Feeling by Bo Burnam (performed by Phoebe Bridgers)

This iteration of The Contract started as my COVID project in 2020. I wanted its setting, The Illumination, not to be an escapist fantasy world but a reflection of our world, where Players could engage with real problems that are relevant today. So it became a world where the powers that be divide the losers and pit them against each other, where we are told every day that the world is about to end, where we feel isolated and hopeless despite being simultaneously closer to everyone else.

The fantasy is the opportunity to fix it. But what does that look like? And what do we have to give up to make the world a better place?

This closeness to real life isn’t always comfortable, but it connects people with their characters and the world in a way that more outlandish settings simply can’t.

Fiction

Yesterday I went online and watched this bartender, Alex, light a cigarette with a snap of his fingers, no lighter. Did it under the observation of a bunch of scientists too, so that’s that. He’s officially Illuminated.

Of course “magic” has always been around. It wasn’t a secret. All those myths, legends, religions, and folktales; they’re all true. Well, sorta. Some of them are make-believe. The rest have been told and retold, twisted each time to suit the monarchy or Disney or what have you.

Nowadays it’s different. Now you’ve got a cell phone with a 4K camera in your pocket. All you’ve gotta do is snap the video, post it to CryptoLeak, and you’ve got millions of views. Just sit back and watch the greediest slime scramble to capitalize.

Never ceases to amaze me. All this technology and people still can’t tell what’s real and what’s a scam.

I hear fire-finger Alex’s bar is doing good business now, but it won’t last. What can he do that anyone with a lighter can’t? No, it won’t be long before the novelty wears off and no one cares about his little party trick. If he’s lucky, some aristocrat or senator will hire him as a Jafar, pay him to stand nearby at parties and press conferences.

The truth is, even if people like Alex are one in fifty thousand, they’re still a dime a dozen.

But you—people like you are different. You’re hungry, curious, and a little vicious. Your untapped potential is off the charts if you play your cards right.

What I'm saying, friend, is you’ve got talent. Real talent. Trust me, I’ve got a knack for spotting that kind of thing.

So, what do you say? Let's shake on it.

Introductions
